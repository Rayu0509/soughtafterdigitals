Contact Tags
  • Tags are labels added to contacts
  • Example: "Hot Lead", "Paid Client", "VIP"
  • Use tags to trigger automations
  • Multiple tags can be on one contact
  • Remove tags to stop automations

Custom Fields
  • Store extra info beyond default fields
  • Examples: "Budget", "Preferred Call Time", "Dog's Name"
  • Can be text, number, date, dropdown
  • Use in email/SMS templates with merge tags
  • Set up in Settings ‚Üí Custom Fields

Why These Matter
  • Personalize messages automatically
  • Segment contacts for targeted campaigns

  • Track lead quality and source

A Typical Sales Pipeline

Stage 1
New Lead
Stage 2
Contacted
Stage 3
Appointment Set
Stage 4
Proposal Sent
Stage 5
Won / Lost
  • 1
    Creating a Pipeline
    Go to Opportunities ‚Üí Pipelines ‚Üí Add Pipeline. Name it, then add stages that match your sales process. You can have multiple pipelines for different services.
  • 2
    Adding Opportunities
    Each contact can have one or more "opportunities" in a pipeline. Add the deal value, estimated close date, and pipeline stage when creating an opportunity.
  • 3
    Moving Leads Through Stages
    Drag and drop opportunities between stages in the Kanban board view. Or use automations to move them automatically based on actions (appointment booked, payment received, etc.).
  • 4
    Won & Lost Tracking
    Mark opportunities as Won or Lost. GHL tracks your conversion rates, average deal size, and revenue ‚Äî giving you data to improve your sales process over time.
